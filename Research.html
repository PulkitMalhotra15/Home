CERN BL4S

Team Leader

BEAMLINE FOR SCHOOLS (BL4S) is an official competition powered by CERN, the European Organization for Nuclear Research, in Geneva, Switzerland. It is open for all high-school students around the world. The competition invites teams of high-school students to propose a scientific experiment that they want to perform.
We competed against 195 teams from 42 countries, to secure a top spot and win a sponsored trip to Geneva, Switzerland and over a span of 15 days, executed our experiment at the T9 beamline of Proton Synchotron.

The project encompassed of:

  • Studying the interaction between charged particles and a magnetic field to learn about the anomalies in the Earth's magnetic field as a function of the variance of the cosmic ray detection rate.
  • Observing muon behavior under varying electromagnetic field strengths to concur the effect of gravitational field on high-atmosphere cosmic muons.
  • Worked with devices like the Delay-Wire Chamber, MicroMegas, Collimators, Scintillators, High-energy electromagnets.
  • Learnt coding in ROOT, modular scientific software toolkit used by CERN and TDAQ, responsible for the online processing of live data, streaming from the ATLAS experiment at the Large Hadron Collider at CERN.
  • Going to publish a paper in IOP (Institute of Physics) regarding the experiment and the information found after data analysis.

University Of Mumbais

Research Intern (2017-2019)

Did a research project under the guidance of Prof Siddharth Kasthurirangan on spectral and pictorial analysis of Total Lunar Eclipse images, gathered on 27 July 2018, in Jaisalmer, Rajasthan.

The project encompassed of:

  • The purpose of the experiment was to study the complex electrostatic environment that differs from the lunar sunlit side to the dark side due to showering of cosmic rays, photons, etc and the flow of lunar dust under the environment. We thus created a model to predict and explain the behaviour.
  • Did analysis of the images taken under various filter detecting different spectrum and polarization.
  • Learnt to use devices and analyze devices like Charge-coupled devices(CCDs), polarization and wavelength filters.
  • Learnt and used Scientific Python Development Environment (SPYDER), which is a scientific environment written in Python, for scientists, engineers and data analysts. It offers a unique combination of a comprehensive development tool with capabilities of a scientific package.
  • Data analysis was done using Anaconda, an open-source software based on Python and R
  • The project is in its final stages and a paper on it is going to be published.
